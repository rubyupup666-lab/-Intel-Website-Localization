A Responsive Timeline of Water Stewardship

Scroll horizontally on large screens. On small screens, the cards stack vertically for easier reading.

1968

Innovation Roots

Intel’s story begins with a focus on invention, engineering, and the future of computing.

This card connects Intel’s early technology history to today’s responsibility to design innovation with environmental awareness.

2020

2030 Vision

Intel announced long-term goals, including a commitment to achieve net positive water by 2030.

The goal connects water conservation, restoration projects, and more efficient operations across communities and facilities.

2024

Measurable Progress

Intel reported continued progress in water conservation and watershed restoration projects.

In 2024, Intel reported conserving about 10.5 billion gallons of water and restoring 2.9 billion gallons through watershed projects.

2026

Community Restoration

Water restoration projects can support rivers, wetlands, agriculture, and local ecosystems.

This milestone highlights the role of partnerships and local watershed work in creating long-term water resilience.

2030

Net Positive Water Goal

By 2030, Intel aims to conserve 60 billion gallons of water and fund restoration projects.

Net positive water means restoring and returning more freshwater than the company consumes in affected watersheds.
